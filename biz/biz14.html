Paydowns fluctuate with sales volume and there is no fixed repayment schedule.

4. Finance vs Auditor – 3-Minute Discussion

Finance Lead: “This is an MCA, not a loan. There’s no fixed repayment schedule, so we don’t need to show it as debt.”

Auditor: “Legally it may not be a loan, but economically it looks like one. Stripe advanced cash and expects recovery.”

Finance Lead: “But repayment only happens if sales occur. If revenue drops, Stripe bears the risk.”

Auditor: “That variability matters. We need to assess whether it’s substantive or merely formal.”

Conclusion: “The accounting hinges on whether there is an unconditional obligation to repay. Substance over form drives the conclusion.”

Discussion Question: What single fact would most influence whether this is treated as debt?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • Existence of unconditional repayment obligation
  • Substantive variability in sales
  • No fixed maturity or minimum payment
  • Provider bears real non-collection risk

6. Should the Effective Interest Rate (EIR) Apply to MCA?

MCA arrangements generally do not have fixed or determinable cash flows at inception. As a result, the effective interest rate method under ASC 835 is typically not applied mechanically.

Key Judgment:
If cash flows become highly predictable and recovery is virtually assured, the arrangement may begin to resemble debt.

Discussion Question: When might an auditor insist on applying an interest model?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • Predictable cash flows
  • Minimal sales volatility
  • Near-certain full recovery
  • Debt-like economic substance

7. ITGC Perspective: Auditing MCA Arrangements

From an ITGC standpoint, MCA repayment is system-driven. Errors in configuration or interfaces can cause large-scale misstatements.

  • Configuration of withholding percentage
  • Completeness of Stripe-to-GL interfaces
  • Automated calculation accuracy (20% / 80%)
  • Change management controls
  • Access restrictions

ITGC Test Procedures – Walkthrough & TOC

Walkthrough

  • Trace one sale from Stripe to GL
  • Verify 20% withholding logic
  • Confirm 80% payout to merchant
