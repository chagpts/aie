1. What Is a Merchant Cash Advance?

A Merchant Cash Advance (MCA) is not structured as a traditional loan. Instead, the provider purchases a portion of the merchant’s future receivables in exchange for an upfront cash payment.

  • No stated interest rate
  • Repayment is tied to actual sales volume
  • No fixed maturity date
  • Payments fluctuate with daily sales

Discussion Question:
Why does the absence of a fixed maturity date matter when assessing whether MCA is debt?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • No unconditional obligation to repay
  • Cash flows are contingent, not time-based
  • Debt requires determinable repayment terms

Discussion Question:
Which feature creates the biggest accounting judgment difference between MCA and a loan?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • Unconditional repayment obligation
  • Exposure to sales variability

3. Is MCA a Loan Under US GAAP?

Under US GAAP, MCA is generally not accounted for as a loan if the substance of the arrangement shows no unconditional obligation to repay.

Auditors apply substance over form. Even without the word “loan,” if repayment is effectively fixed, MCA may be recharacterized as debt.

Discussion Question:
What evidence would an auditor look for to challenge non-debt treatment?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • Predictable and stable sales history
  • Paydown structure ensuring full recovery
  • Lack of meaningful downside risk to provider

4. Debate Topic: Loan Regulation vs. MCA

Finance Team Lead:
Loans are heavily regulated. MCA is a purchase of future receivables, not a loan.

Auditor:
Legal form matters less than economic substance under US GAAP.

Conclusion:
Regulatory classification and accounting classification are separate analyses.

Discussion Question:
Why can an arrangement be non-loan legally but still treated as debt for accounting?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • Accounting focuses on cash flow obligations
  • Legal structure does not override GAAP principles

5. Does MCA Create Financing Cost?

Yes. Even without stated interest, MCA embeds an implied financing cost.

Discussion Question:
Why is financing cost recognized even when no interest rate is stated?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • Time value of money still exists
  • Provider expects recovery in excess of cash advanced

6. Should the Effective Interest Rate (EIR) Method Apply to MCA?

The EIR method is generally not mechanically applied because cash flows are not fixed or determinable at inception.

Discussion Question:
Under what circumstances might an auditor insist on applying an effective interest model?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • When repayment becomes highly predictable
  • When variability is no longer substantive
