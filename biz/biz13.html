ITGC English Study

Topic: System Development & Implementation (Initiation Phase)

1. Warm-up & ITGC Overview

Icebreaker Discussion

  • Have you ever been involved in a system implementation or ERP project?
  • What usually goes wrong in system implementation projects?

Key ITGC Concepts

  • IT General Controls (ITGC)
  • System Development Life Cycle (SDLC)
  • Initiation Phase
  • Governance and approval
  • Investment performance evaluation
  • Project Management Office (PMO)

Discussion

  • Why is the initiation phase critical from an audit perspective?
  • What risks arise when management approval is weak or informal?

2. Control 1.1.1

Control Statement:
New system implementation should be approved through appropriate review and approval procedures.

Audit Objective

To ensure that new information systems are implemented based on formal review, business justification, and management approval.

Key Risks

  • Systems implemented without clear business justification
  • Absence of investment performance or ROI evaluation
  • Vendor selection based on relationships rather than capability

Interview Practice (Role Play)

Auditor: Thank you for your time today. I’d like to ask a few questions regarding the approval process for the new system implementation.

Manager: Sure. I’ll do my best to explain.

Auditor: First, is there a formal policy for approving new system implementations?

Manager: Yes. We have an IT investment and system approval policy. Any new system requires a formal review and approval before the project starts.

Auditor: Who has the authority to approve a new system under that policy?

Manager: The final approval is granted by the executive committee, including the CIO and CFO. For large projects, board-level approval is also required.

Auditor: Was an investment performance evaluation conducted before this system was approved?

Manager: Yes. We prepared a business case that included expected cost savings, efficiency improvements, and estimated return on investment.

Auditor: Was that evaluation documented and formally reviewed?

Manager: Yes, it was documented and reviewed during the approval meeting. The results were included in the approval materials.

Auditor: Did management review benchmarking materials or case studies from other companies?

Manager: Yes. We reviewed implementation cases from several large companies in the same industry, including both successful and unsuccessful projects.

Auditor: How was the vendor selected, and was prior experience with large enterprises considered?

Manager: We conducted a vendor evaluation process. The vendor was selected based on technical capability, project methodology, and experience implementing similar systems for large enterprises.

Auditor: Were those evaluation results documented and approved?

Manager: Yes. The evaluation results and selection rationale were documented and approved as part of the procurement process.

Auditor: Thank you. We will review the related documents to confirm what we discussed today.

Manager: No problem. Please let me know if you need any additional information.

Document Review Practice

Documents to review:
  • Policy for IT investment and system approval
  • Management or board approval records
  • Business case or ROI analysis documents
  • Vendor selection and evaluation materials
  • Contract approval documents
Discussion points:
  • Which documents clearly demonstrate management approval?
  • What evidence would be considered insufficient for audit purposes?
  • Is post-implementation evaluation required under ITGC?

3. Control 1.1.2

Control Statement:
Effective governance and communication structures should exist during system development.

Audit Objective

To ensure that appropriate governance and communication mechanisms are established to support effective system development.

Key Concepts

  • Project Management Office (PMO)
  • Steering committee
  • Stakeholders
  • Cross-functional participation
  • Communication and escalation procedures

Discussion

  • Why is a PMO important from an audit perspective?
  • What risks exist if business users are not involved in the project?

Interview Practice

Auditor: Thank you for meeting with us. I’d like to ask a few questions about the governance structure during the system implementation project.

Manager: Sure. Please go ahead.

Auditor: Was a PMO or steering committee established for the project?

Manager: Yes. A formal PMO was established at the beginning of the project, and a steering committee was also formed for major decisions.

Auditor: What was the role of the PMO in the project?

Manager: The PMO was responsible for project planning, progress monitoring, issue tracking, and coordination among departments.

Auditor: How were issues, risks, and key decisions escalated during the project?

Manager: Operational issues were handled by the project team, while major risks and decisions were escalated to the steering committee through regular status meetings.

Auditor: Were all relevant departments involved in the project?

Manager: Yes. The project included representatives from IT, accounting, finance, and internal control, as well as key business users.

Auditor: How were roles and responsibilities defined?

Manager: Roles and responsibilities were clearly defined in a RACI matrix and documented in the project governance charter.

Auditor: Can you provide evidence such as meeting minutes or governance documents?

Manager: Yes. We have PMO meeting minutes, steering committee materials, and the project governance documents available for review.

Auditor: Who had the final decision-making authority for the project?

Manager: Final decision-making authority rested with the steering committee, chaired by the CIO, with approval from the CFO for financial matters.

Auditor: Thank you. We will review the documents and follow up if we have additional questions.

Manager: Of course. Please let us know if anything else is needed.

Document Review

Documents:
  • Project governance charter
  • PMO or steering committee organization chart
  • RACI matrix
  • System development methodology documents
  • Meeting minutes and issue logs
Red flags to discuss:
  • No formal PMO or steering committee
  • Limited involvement of business departments
  • Unclear roles and responsibilities

4. Audit Simulation Exercise

Scenario:
A company implemented a new accounting system in the prior year.

Provided evidence:
  • No formal ROI or investment performance analysis
  • Vendor selected primarily based on prior relationships
  • PMO existed, but meeting minutes were not retained
  • Approval obtained via email only, without formal sign-off
Group tasks:
  • Identify control deficiencies
  • Assess control effectiveness (effective, partially effective, ineffective)
  • Draft audit findings in English

Example audit finding:
The company did not perform a formal investment performance evaluation prior to system implementation, increasing the risk that the system may not align with business objectives.

5. Audit Findings & Wrap-up

Communicating Audit Findings in English

Standard structure:

  • Condition
  • Criteria
  • Cause
  • Effect
  • Recommendation

Useful audit phrases:

  • Based on our inquiry and document review
  • We noted that management did not
  • This increases the risk that
  • We recommend that management establish

Closing Discussion

  • Which control is most difficult to implement in practice?
  • How might management challenge audit findings?
  • How should auditors respond professionally?
