Merchant Cash Advance (MCA) – Accounting & Audit Study

Reference: https://stripe.com/resources/more/merchant-cash-advance

1. What Is a Merchant Cash Advance (MCA)?

An MCA is a financing arrangement where a merchant receives cash upfront in exchange for a percentage of future card sales. Repayment occurs only when sales occur.

  • No fixed repayment schedule
  • No stated interest rate
  • Repayment fluctuates with sales volume

Discussion Question:
Is MCA closer to a loan or a sale of future receivables?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • Focus on economic substance, not label
  • No unconditional obligation to repay
  • Cash provider bears sales risk

2. MCA vs Factoring

Item MCA Factoring
Underlying Asset Future sales Existing receivables
Repayment Variable Fixed
Risk Transfer Shared / Provider bears risk Often with recourse

Discussion Question:
Why is MCA generally not treated as factoring under US GAAP?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • No identified receivable at inception
  • Sales have not yet occurred
  • Cash flows are contingent

3. Is MCA a Loan under US GAAP?

Under US GAAP, MCA is generally not classified as a loan if there is no unconditional obligation to repay cash.

Key test: Is the merchant required to repay the advance even if sales stop?

Discussion Question:
What feature most clearly distinguishes MCA from debt?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • No fixed maturity
  • No minimum payment
  • Repayment tied solely to sales

4. Regulatory Debate: Loan vs MCA (3-Minute Conversation)

Finance Lead:
“We’re planning to use an MCA. Since it’s not a loan, we don’t need to show debt, right?”

Auditor:
“Labels don’t matter. We need to assess whether, in substance, this creates a debt obligation.”

Finance Lead:
“But loans are regulated lending products. MCA isn’t subject to lending laws.”

Auditor:
“Regulatory treatment helps, but accounting focuses on whether repayment is unavoidable.”

Discussion Question:
Should regulatory classification override accounting substance?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • Accounting is independent from legal regulation
  • Regulation is supportive, not determinative
  • Substance-over-form principle applies

5. Financing Costs in MCA

Although MCA has no stated interest, the difference between cash received and expected total payback represents a financing component.

Discussion Question:
Can an MCA have financing cost without interest?

Model Answer (Key Bullets)
  • Yes, implicit financing exists
  • No separate interest expense recognized
  • Cost is embedded in sales remittance
